City of Nizhny Novgorod, Russia

Overview and History

Nizhny Novgorod is Russia's fifth largest city, ranking after Moscow, Saint Petersburg, Novosibirsk and Yekaterinburg. The population of Nizhny Novgorod is about 1,268,000 (last known at 2015) and the area of the City is 178 sq. miles (411 sq. km).

Nizhny Novgorod is the administrative center (capital) of the Volga Federal District and the head of Nizhegorodskaya region. The city is located 260 miles (420 km) east of Moscow. It stands at the confluence of two major waterways of the European part of Russia - the Oka and Volga rivers. This location made the city an important shipping center of the country. The Oka river divides the city into two parts.

In 1221 Russian Prince Yuri Vsevolodovich of Vladimir founded a wooden fort at the confluence of the Volga and the Oka rivers. The settlement was named Nizhny Novgorod. It's name literally means Newtown the Lower, to distinguish it from the older Novgorod. In 1350, the town became the capital of an independent Suzdal-Nizhny Novgorod principality, which occupied a vast territory and competed with the Moscow principality. During this period, Nizhny Novgorod region was actively populated. Later on in 1425 Suzdal-Nizhny Novgorod principality was annexed to the Moscow principality. In 1817, Makarevskaya Fair moved to Nizhny Novgorod attracting millions of visitors annually. By the middle of the 19th century, the town was known as the trading capital of the Russian Empire. From 1932 to 1990 Nizhny Novgorod was called Gorky in honor of the famous Russian writer Maxim Gorky. The “closed” status of Gorky City came to the end in 1990, when the original name Nizhny Novgorod was returned to the City. Read more historical facts

Climate

The climate in the region is humid continental and it is similar to the climate in Moscow, although colder in winter, which lasts from late November until late March with a permanent snow cover. The average temperature in January ism minus 8.9 degrees Celsius, in July - plus 19.4 degrees Celsius. The months June, July and August have a nice average temperature.

Cold season / winter is in the months January, February, March and December. On average, the warmest month is July. On average, the coolest month is January. Read more about the weather

What kind of clothes should you pack?

If you are coming to Nizhny Novgorod from a warm climate, especially if you are arriving in the winter (December–March), be sure to bring warm clothing.

  • Light-weight clothing is best for summer (June, July, August)
  • Sweaters and light waterproof jackets are useful for cool and rainy days in the spring and fall (March, April, May, September, October, November)
  • Heavy jackets, scarves, gloves, hats and waterproof boots are essential for the winter (December-March)

You can buy bulkier or heavier items once you have arrived, especially if you have a luggage allowance.

Dining

Gastronomic scales of Nizhny Novgorod are truly limitless. Each guest of the city will find here a restaurant or a cafe that will meet his budget and culinary preferences. The maximum concentration of restaurants is on the Bolshaya Pokrovskaya Street, which runs all way from the Nizhny Novgorod Kremlin down to Maxim Gorky Square. Another popular place is the Rozhdestvenskaya street between the Square of National Unity and the oldest in the city the Blagoveschensky Monastery.

  • Gourmet food

    In restaurants designed for gourmets, a fashionable European trend is well traced.

    The best steaks in the city are prepared in the restaurant Mitrich steakhouse(Kovalikhinskaya st., 8) Here you will find an interesting author's cuisine, stylish interior, vintage furniture and excellent service. Among other favorites - a warm salad with eel, cream soup of white mushrooms, a dampened duck leg.

    Restaurant De Kas(Maxima Gorkogo str., 121) specializes in Italian and French cuisine. Its interior is in an European style, and only one quick look at the menu makes the rapid heart rate of true gourmets. What to choose - a duck with porcini mushrooms with raspberry sauce, smoked pork belly with fried chanterelles, leg of lamb with baked plums or something else?

  • Russian Food

    In a city with an ancient history it is a quite logical to give a favor to restaurants with traditional Russian cuisine and an appropriate interior, where everything is for your enjoy: from the food and to the price.

    The restaurant Usadba(Osharskaya str., 10/30) is filled with the atmosphere of the noble life of the 19th century - silk-covered walls, soft sofas, screens, lampshades, bronze candlesticks, a fireplace, bearskin on the floor and servers dressed in livery. From delicacies - veal side with mustard sauce, stewed chicken with ceps and sour cream, cold assorted home made pickles.

    When going into Pjatkin(Rozhdestvenskaya St, 25), you feel as if you are in another century. The atmosphere is unique, as you can sit on a comfortable armchair and look out of the window to the Rozhdestvenskaya Street. There you can go in order to taste the traditional Russian cuisine or simply to drink a liqueur of their own production

  • International Food

    For those who do not want to change their gastronomic habits or conversely, wants to diversify the menu, there is a direct road to restaurants with international cuisine, where you can find dishes from all over the world.

    The restaurant of southern cuisine "Ugolek" (Rozhdestvenskaya St., 43) has everything for enjoy of Georgian and Caucasian cuisine.

    Jewish and German cuisine - the specialization of a tavern "Red Sara" (Sergievskaya street, 13) located in the mini-hotel with the same name.

    The special food of the Azerbaijani restaurant "Khurma" (B. Pecherskaya st., 26) - all kinds of lamb, Caspian sturgeon and vegetables cooked on the grill.

    Tea house "Tyubeteika" (Rozhdestvenskaya St., 45) is popular with Uzbek chef - master of shurpa, kebabs and pilaf.

    In the "Happy Kuma" (Kostina St., 3) you can try Ukrainian vareniki, borsch, Kiev cutlets.

Sights of Interests

There are more than 600 unique historic, architectural, and cultural monuments in Nizhny Novgorod. The historic center of the city, despite significant restructuring, has a significant number of historic civil buildings of the 18th - early 20th centuries, including a lot of monuments of wooden architecture.
